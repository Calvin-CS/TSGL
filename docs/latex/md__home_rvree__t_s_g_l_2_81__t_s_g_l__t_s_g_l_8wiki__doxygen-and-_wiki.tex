{\itshape {\bfseries{B\+IG N\+O\+TE\+:}}} You {\itshape {\bfseries{N\+E\+ED}}} doxygen 1.\+8.\+6 in order to update the documentation correctly. Previous versions will cause a {\itshape {\bfseries{L\+OT}}} of problems.

In order to provide an A\+PI and extensive documentation for the T\+S\+GL library, we rely on a tool known as \href{http://www.stack.nl/~dimitri/doxygen/}{\texttt{ Doxygen}}.

We also use the \href{https://github.com/Calvin-CS/TSGL/wiki}{\texttt{ Wiki}} pages to provide important information about the T\+S\+GL library (Debian and R\+PM packages, F\+A\+Qs, etc...).

If you take a look at our \href{http://calvin-cs.github.io/TSGL/html/pages.html}{\texttt{ Doxygen}} pages, you\textquotesingle{}ll notice that our Wiki pages also show up as well as the documentation for the T\+S\+GL code.

Which means, in order to keep these two up-\/to-\/date, whenever you make a change to the Wiki pages, you\textquotesingle{}ll have to also update the Doxygen pages.

If you only added new documentation to the T\+S\+GL source code, then you do {\itshape {\bfseries{N\+OT}}} have to update the Wiki pages. You simply have to update the Doxygen pages.

We provide two different processes for each situation.
